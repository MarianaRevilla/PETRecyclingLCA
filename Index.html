Assessing the Potential Environmental Benefits of Increased Recycled PET Waste through Environmental Awareness Programs

This Life Cycle Assessment was submitted as a final dissertation for the Degree of Master of Science in Corporate Environmental Management at the University of Surrey, Guildford, United Kingdom in October 2020. The dissertation achieved a distinction award and may be fully accessed here. Below is a brief summary of the assessment.

INTRODUCTION

As awareness on the environmental impact of plastic packaging increases, consumers look for more sustainable alternatives for waste disposal. Recycling has emerged as one of the best waste management options; its environmental impact is considered to be less significant than that of more traditional alternatives. Life Cycle Assessment is used to holistically evaluate the environmental impact of a product system across different impact categories. The present assessment focuses on two different disposal scenarios for waste PET bottles by evaluating the complete Life Cycle. The first scenario considers a business-as-usual scenario for waste PET bottle disposal in Mexico, while the second one considers an increase in post-consumer bottle sorting and closed-loop recycling due to the implementation of environmental awareness programs. What would be the environmental benefit of increasing the sorting and recycling of waste PET bottles? The obtained results show that an increase in recycling decreases the environmental impact across all selected category impacts, proving the effectiveness of environmental awareness programs. The Life Cycle Assessment results are used to quantify the environmental benefit of increased recycling rates.

METHODOLOGY

Life Cycle Assessment (LCA) is a methodology used to holistically evaluate the environmental impact of different waste disposal scenarios. Since LCA is useful for scenario comparison, it may be used to quantify the environmental impact of an increased rate of recycling through environmental awareness programs. The LCA compares a scenario in which a waste PET bottle follows the waste disposal scenario in Mexico (landfilling and incineration) with a second scenario in which bottles are sorted, collected and recycled through closed-loop technologies. The LCA was developed following the guidelines established by the ISO 14040:2006 through four steps. The selected impact categories represent the critical impacts associated with the Life Cycle of a PET bottle at a local and global level. The impact categories were assessed through ReCiPe 2016, which was considered suitable since its indicators were developed at a global level. The hierarchical point of view was selected; it is based on scientific consensus and considers that nature's limits must be respected. The background was modelled using Ecoinvent version 3, while the data for the foreground was obtained from offical government documents.

RESULTS

By comparing the two scenarios, the impact on the environment of an intervention to collect and recycle waste PET bottles may be quantified. The results tab shows the characterization results of the assessment. All of the impact categories show that closed-loop recycling has a more positive impact on the environment than the traditional waste management scenario in Mexico. In the calculator tab, the environmental impact of recycling PET may be quantified, and the positive effects of sorting and recycling may be seen. The graphs are interactive and were created using Javascript.
